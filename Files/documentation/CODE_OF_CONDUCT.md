# Code of Conduct

**Version**: 2.0  
**Effective**: January 13, 2026

---

## Our Pledge

We, the contributors and maintainers of the AgenticCoder project, are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all people, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or other dimensions of diversity.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Being Respectful**: Treat all community members with respect and kindness
- **Using Inclusive Language**: Avoid language that is offensive, exclusionary, or demeaning
- **Being Constructive**: Provide helpful, constructive feedback and criticism
- **Showing Empathy**: Be empathetic toward differing opinions and experiences
- **Assuming Good Intent**: Assume others are acting with good intent
- **Accepting Responsibility**: Take responsibility for our words and actions
- **Focusing on What's Best**: Focus on what is best for the community, not individual interests

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassing or discriminatory comments or behavior
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, or demeaning remarks
- Personal attacks
- Publishing others' private information
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Violent threats or violent language
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **All community spaces**: Issues, discussions, pull requests, comments
- **Project repositories**: Including all branches and forks
- **Project events**: Conferences, meetups, webinars, workshops
- **Public communications**: Social media, forums, blogs about the project
- **Unofficial channels**: Community-organized discussions about the project

The Code of Conduct also applies to individuals representing the project in professional settings, such as conference presentations or community outreach.

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it immediately.

### How to Report

**For serious or urgent issues**:
1. Email [conduct@example.com](mailto:conduct@example.com) with:
   - Your name and contact info (if comfortable sharing)
   - Description of incident
   - Names of people involved (if known)
   - Witnesses (if any)
   - Links to relevant discussions/comments

**For public discussions**:
1. Use the "Report" button on GitHub
2. Email maintainers with details

**For maintainers/team members**:
1. Contact the Code of Conduct committee directly
2. Email [conduct@example.com](mailto:conduct@example.com)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be:
- **Treated confidentially**: Shared only with those responsible for response
- **Handled professionally**: Reviewed fairly and promptly
- **Documented**: Kept for records (not public)

Your identity and safety will be protected. We will not tolerate retaliation against reporters.

## Enforcement

### Process

1. **Initial Review** (24 hours):
   - Committee receives report
   - Preliminary assessment
   - If emergency, take immediate protective action

2. **Investigation** (5-7 days):
   - Gather facts from all parties
   - Consult community guidelines
   - Determine if violation occurred

3. **Resolution** (within 14 days):
   - If violation found: Determine appropriate action
   - If no violation: Close case
   - Communicate decision to reporter (if appropriate)

4. **Appeal** (7 days):
   - Reporter or respondent may appeal
   - Different committee member reviews
   - Final decision made within 7 days

### Possible Actions

**For minor violations**:
- Private warning
- Public apology (if appropriate)
- Comments removed

**For moderate violations**:
- Temporary ban from specific channels (1-30 days)
- Requirement to modify behavior
- Public statement of violation

**For severe violations**:
- Permanent ban from project
- Public statement of violation
- Legal action (if appropriate)

### Examples

**Minor Violation**: Using exclusionary language, dismissive tone

**Moderate Violation**: Repeated disrespect after warning, personal attacks

**Severe Violation**: Harassment, threats, discrimination, repeated violations

---

## Rights of Respondents

If you are accused of violating the Code of Conduct:

- **Right to know**: You'll be told what violation is alleged
- **Right to respond**: You can provide your perspective
- **Right to fair process**: Investigation will be fair and impartial
- **Right to appeal**: You can appeal the decision

## Responsibilities

### Community Members

- **Contribute positively**: Be kind and respectful to others
- **Report issues**: Report unacceptable behavior
- **Educate**: Help others understand the Code of Conduct
- **Include others**: Welcome people from all backgrounds

### Maintainers

- **Enforce fairly**: Apply Code of Conduct consistently
- **Be transparent**: Communicate decisions clearly
- **Educate**: Help community understand expectations
- **Lead by example**: Model the behavior we expect

### Code of Conduct Committee

- **Investigate promptly**: Review reports quickly
- **Decide fairly**: Make unbiased decisions
- **Communicate clearly**: Explain actions and reasoning
- **Improve process**: Update procedure as needed
